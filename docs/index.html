Freally Capture

Record and stream like a studio — scenes, sources, multistream, one clean app.

A local-first, cross-platform live-streaming & recording studio (OBS-class): a real-time GPU scene compositor, multi-track recording, and multistream to every platform at once — entirely on your machine. No accounts, no telemetry, no cloud.

🎬

Owned GPU compositor

Build scenes from sources — display, window, webcam, browser, media — and compose them live on the GPU (wgpu) with per-source filters and transitions.

📡

Multistream, direct

Go live to Twitch, YouTube, Kick and more — several at once — over RTMP/RTMPS/SRT/WHIP, straight from your machine. No restream server, completely free.

⏺️

Multi-track recording

Record with your GPU's hardware encoder (NVENC/Quick Sync/AMF/VAAPI/VideoToolbox) — or the owned lossless freally-video codec — on up to 6 audio tracks.

📷

Virtual camera

Turn your produced scene into a webcam for Zoom, Meet, or Discord — all on-device.

⏪

Replay buffer

Keep a rolling buffer and save the last N seconds with a hotkey — cross-vendor, on the owned codec, without interrupting your stream.

🔒

Local-first, no account

No login, no telemetry, no cloud. The only thing that leaves your machine is the stream you chose to send. First-class on Windows, macOS & Linux.

In development — building toward 0.70.0 (studio MVP)

Capture is in (0.25.0): screens, windows, and webcams stream live into the program preview on Windows (DXGI + Windows.Graphics.Capture), macOS (ScreenCaptureKit), and Linux (the ScreenCast portal on Wayland — the system dialog picks the source — plus a direct X11 path). The owned wgpu GPU compositor, the scene/source model, and the on-GPU filter chain are in (0.40.0), and the owned audio engine mixes mic + desktop audio across up to 6 tracks with the owned classic-DSP filters (spectral denoise — no ML — plus gate, compressor, limiter, EQ, gain, ducking), monitoring, push-to-talk, and a LUFS meter. Recording has just landed (0.55.0) — the owned freally-video lossless codec, hardware encoders (NVENC/Quick Sync/AMF/VAAPI/VideoToolbox) + x264/x265/AV1 fallback, multi-track mp4/mkv/mov/webm with file splitting and pause/resume, HEVC/AV1 + remux-to-mp4, and a Media source — with the patent-encumbered wire codecs driven through the clearly-labeled, on-demand, hash-verified ffmpeg bridge (never bundled). The preview now feels like OBS (0.56.0): a native GPU preview paints the compositor's frame straight to the screen with no read-back/encode round-trip — Windows (DirectComposition), macOS (CAMetalLayer), and Linux (X11/Vulkan) — with the selection box + transform handles drawn into the GPU frame; the JPEG preview stays as the universal fallback (and covers Wayland). Window Capture picks with live thumbnails, lists minimized windows, re-binds to the same window across a restart, and auto-recovers. The studio MVP (0.70.0) is here: single-target streaming to Twitch/YouTube/Kick/Facebook/Trovo over RTMP/RTMPS with auto-reconnect, Studio Mode (preview/program) with cut/fade/slide/swipe/luma transitions, global hotkeys, profiles + scene collections, and a real fps/CPU/GPU stats dock — the local recording keeps running no matter what the stream does. Grab the latest build from the download menu above.

What the first public build will do

  • Build scenes from sources (display, window, webcam, capture card, browser, media, image, text, color, audio) on an owned wgpu GPU compositor, with per-source filters (chroma key, color correction, LUT, blur, sharpen).
  • Record multi-track to mp4/mkv/mov/webm via hardware encoders (NVENC/Quick Sync/AMF/VAAPI/VideoToolbox) with an x264 fallback — or fully lossless in the owned freally-video format.
  • Go live single-target over RTMP/RTMPS to Twitch/YouTube/Kick/Facebook/Trovo/custom, with auto-reconnect and a configurable stream delay.
  • Studio Mode (preview/program) with cut/fade/slide/stinger transitions, a virtual camera output, global hotkeys, profiles + scene collections, and a live stats dock.
  • No account, no telemetry, no cloud — your stream goes direct to the platform.

Simultaneous multistream to several platforms, SRT/WHIP, nested scenes and source groups, the replay-buffer presets, and the WebSocket remote-control API arrive in the 0.85.0 and 1.0.0 milestones. The virtual camera lands as its own signed-driver milestone right after 0.70.0. Every build is an honest snapshot — capture, scenes, the GPU compositor with filters, the audio mixer, multi-track recording, opt-in P2P remote guests, and the streaming + Studio-Mode MVP (0.70.0) all work; no account, no telemetry, no cloud.
