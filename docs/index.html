Freally Capture

Record and stream like a studio — scenes, sources, multistream, one clean app.

A local-first, cross-platform live-streaming & recording studio (OBS-class): a real-time GPU scene compositor, multi-track recording, and multistream to every platform at once — entirely on your machine. No accounts, no telemetry, no cloud.

🎬

Owned GPU compositor

Build scenes from sources — display, window, webcam, browser, media — and compose them live on the GPU (wgpu) with per-source filters and transitions.

📡

Multistream, direct

Go live to Twitch, YouTube, Kick and more — several at once — over RTMP/RTMPS/SRT/WHIP, straight from your machine. No restream server, completely free.

⏺️

Multi-track recording

Record with your GPU's hardware encoder (NVENC/Quick Sync/AMF/VAAPI/VideoToolbox) — or the owned lossless freally-video codec — on up to 6 audio tracks.

📷

Virtual camera

Turn your produced scene into a webcam for Zoom, Meet, or Discord — all on-device.

⏪

Replay buffer

Keep a rolling buffer and save the last N seconds with a hotkey — cross-vendor, on the owned codec, without interrupting your stream.

🔒

Local-first, no account

No login, no telemetry, no cloud. The only thing that leaves your machine is the stream you chose to send. First-class on Windows, macOS & Linux.

Latest — sources, devices & calibration (0.99.0)

Capture is in (0.25.0): screens, windows, and webcams stream live into the program preview on Windows (DXGI + Windows.Graphics.Capture), macOS (ScreenCaptureKit), and Linux (the ScreenCast portal on Wayland — the system dialog picks the source — plus a direct X11 path). The owned wgpu GPU compositor, the scene/source model, and the on-GPU filter chain are in (0.40.0), and the owned audio engine mixes mic + desktop audio across up to 6 tracks with the owned classic-DSP filters (spectral denoise — no ML — plus gate, compressor, limiter, EQ, gain, ducking), monitoring, push-to-talk, and a LUFS meter. Recording has just landed (0.55.0) — the owned freally-video lossless codec, hardware encoders (NVENC/Quick Sync/AMF/VAAPI/VideoToolbox) + x264/x265/AV1 fallback, multi-track mp4/mkv/mov/webm with file splitting and pause/resume, HEVC/AV1 + remux-to-mp4, and a Media source — with the patent-encumbered wire codecs driven through the clearly-labeled, on-demand, hash-verified ffmpeg bridge (never bundled). The preview now feels like OBS (0.56.0): a native GPU preview paints the compositor's frame straight to the screen with no read-back/encode round-trip — Windows (DirectComposition), macOS (CAMetalLayer), and Linux (X11/Vulkan) — with the selection box + transform handles drawn into the GPU frame; the JPEG preview stays as the universal fallback (and covers Wayland). Window Capture picks with live thumbnails, lists minimized windows, re-binds to the same window across a restart, and auto-recovers. The studio MVP (0.70.0) is here: single-target streaming to Twitch/YouTube/Kick/Facebook/Trovo over RTMP/RTMPS with auto-reconnect, Studio Mode (preview/program) with cut/fade/slide/swipe/luma transitions, global hotkeys, profiles + scene collections, and a real fps/CPU/GPU stats dock — the local recording keeps running no matter what the stream does. Streaming depth shipped (0.85.0): simultaneous multistream to several platforms at once, SRT/WHIP, a vertical 9:16 canvas recorded/streamed independently, nested scenes + source groups + per-scene audio, a rolling replay buffer, high-FPS/4K/Rec.709 encoder depth, and a live chat overlay + floating reactions that need no key or sign-in. The extensibility surface shipped (0.90.0): a password-protected WebSocket remote-control API (Stream Deck / Companion), browser docks, sandboxed Lua scripting, and a plugin SDK — all local, off by default. Distribution and per-app audio have landed (0.95.0): a signed self-hosted auto-updater that refuses any package it can't verify against a key baked into the binary, per-application audio capture with its own mixer strip (Windows-first, via WASAPI process loopback), an NDI runtime seam that detects — never bundles — a user-installed runtime, a first-run EULA gate, and pause/resume for video played into the stream. Game Capture is present as an honestly-flagged seam: the app never injects a GPU hook silently, and it tells you the anti-cheat risk before it would. The launch polish has landed (0.96.0): the studio speaks 18 languages and follows your system language on a fresh install (Arabic reads right-to-left); it is fully operable from the keyboard and audible to a screen reader, which announces going live, recording, reconnecting, and dropped frames; a first-run wizard probes your GPUs and cores and proposes an encoder, canvas, and bitrate with the reason stated in your own language; and there is a Ctrl/Cmd-K command palette, one Settings modal, an About page, and light and custom themes. The first must-have batch has landed (0.97.0): multi-step undo/redo behind every scene edit, an OBS scene-collection importer with an honest per-source report, a missing-file doctor, a full multi-select with align/distribute and pull-out guides, precise copy-paste transforms, a keying workbench with a matte view, a multiview monitor, fullscreen projectors for any scene/source/program/preview on any display, and a one-key still grab. The second must-have batch has landed (0.98.0) — broadcast safety & reliability: a go-live pre-flight checklist, always-on safety alarms (silent audio, clipping, a black/frozen picture, a low-disk forecast), a source health dashboard, a mid-session encoder failover that keeps the show up when a driver resets, crash-safe mp4/mov recording with a next-launch repair, a quit guard that finalizes everything in order, a panic button that cuts to a privacy slate and hard-mutes everything, token-based recording filename templates, and a redacted diagnostics bundle that never includes a secret and never sends anything. The third and final must-have batch has landed (0.99.0) — sources, devices & calibration: built-in test signals (SMPTE bars, a calibration grid, a motion sweep, a lineup tone, and an A/V sync flash+beep pattern), a guided A/V sync calibration workbench that measures your camera/mic offset and applies it, timer & clock sources (wall clock, countdown with at-zero actions, stopwatch, time since live/recording), text sources bound to a watched .txt/CSV/JSON file, a searchable hotkey map with conflict detection and an exportable cheat sheet, mixer pan/solo (PFL)/mono, per-device deinterlacing, and cross-platform camera controls (exposure, white balance, focus, zoom, gain) with per-device profiles that survive hotplug and restarts. What the first public build will do

  • Build scenes from sources (display, window, webcam, capture card, browser, media, image, text, color, audio) on an owned wgpu GPU compositor, with per-source filters (chroma key, color correction, LUT, blur, sharpen).
  • Record multi-track to mp4/mkv/mov/webm via hardware encoders (NVENC/Quick Sync/AMF/VAAPI/VideoToolbox) with an x264 fallback — or fully lossless in the owned freally-video format.
  • Go live single-target over RTMP/RTMPS to Twitch/YouTube/Kick/Facebook/Trovo/custom, with auto-reconnect and a configurable stream delay.
  • Studio Mode (preview/program) with cut/fade/slide/stinger transitions, a virtual camera output, global hotkeys, profiles + scene collections, and a live stats dock.
  • No account, no telemetry, no cloud — your stream goes direct to the platform.

Simultaneous multistream to several platforms, SRT/WHIP, nested scenes and source groups, the replay-buffer presets, and the WebSocket remote-control API have all shipped (0.85.0 and 0.90.0, both published in 0.95.0). The virtual camera, a true browser source, and game-capture GPU hooking each land as their own milestones. Every build is an honest snapshot — capture, scenes, the GPU compositor with filters, the audio mixer, multi-track recording, opt-in P2P remote guests, the streaming + Studio-Mode MVP (0.70.0), and signed auto-update all work; no account, no telemetry, no cloud.
